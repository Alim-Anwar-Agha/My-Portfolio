The morning of September 1st, 2025 felt different. I had been waiting for this day for months — the day I would officially become a student at the **University of Engineering and Technology, Faisalabad**. But standing at the gate with my admission letter in hand, all that excitement turned into a quiet nervousness.

I am Alim Anwar, registered as **2025-BSCPE-168**, enrolled in the BS Computer Engineering program. And this is where my story begins.

## The Campus, The Crowd

Walking into UET Faisalabad for the first time as a student felt overwhelming in the best possible way. There were students everywhere — seniors who walked like they owned the place, freshmen like me who were trying hard not to look lost, and faculty members moving between departments with purpose.

I had chosen Computer Engineering because I genuinely believed it sat at the intersection of everything I found interesting — hardware, software, logic, and problem-solving. But on day one, all of that sounded very far away. The immediate concern was simpler: *find your classroom, find your seat, don't be late.*

## The First Timetable

When I got my course timetable, two subjects stood out immediately:

- **Programming Fundamentals (PF)**
- **Other core engineering courses**

I had heard about Programming Fundamentals from seniors. Some called it the most important course of the first semester. "If you understand PF properly," one senior told me, "everything else in CS and CE becomes easier." I did not fully understand what he meant yet — but I would soon find out.

## First Impressions of the Department

The Computer Engineering department had a focused energy to it. Students in upper semesters were already talking about projects, lab work, and research. I overheard a group mention a faculty member whose work in **Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning** had been published internationally. That name — Dr. Bilal Ahmad — would come up again in the days ahead.

At the time, I noted it the way you note any name early in university: filed away, not fully processed yet.

## What I Was Feeling

Honestly? A mix of everything.

Excited about what I was going to learn. Nervous about whether I could keep up. Curious about the people around me — who would become friends, who would be in my study groups, who would push me to be better.

University is not just about courses and grades. The first day reminded me of that. It is about stepping into an environment that challenges you in ways you did not expect.

## Looking Back Now

Sitting here writing this, I can say that first day was the smallest possible version of everything that followed. The confusion, the curiosity, the slow realization of how much there was to learn — all of it was already present on day one, just in seed form.

The journey from that gate to where I am now passed through lectures, late-night study sessions, frustrating bugs in code, database schemas that refused to make sense, and slowly — very slowly — moments where things actually clicked.

This blog is about all of that. Fifteen posts, one honest journey.
